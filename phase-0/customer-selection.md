# Customer Selection and Qualification

## Ideal Phase 0 customer

A restaurant qualifies when most of the following are true:

- One operating location or one clearly authorised pilot location
- Identifiable decision-maker reachable through WhatsApp or email
- At least 30 public reviews
- Approximately 4–10 new reviews per month or more
- Recent unanswered or weakly answered reviews
- Public menu, phone number, address, and hours available for comparison
- No mature reputation-management product already embedded
- Willingness to provide 10–15 minutes of onboarding input
- Willingness to approve and manually publish responses
- Authority to confirm or correct listing information

## Exclusions for the initial cohort

Do not recruit initially when:

- The restaurant is closed, unstable, or changing ownership.
- A franchise head office controls every reply and listing field.
- No one can establish who controls the public listings.
- The restaurant expects guaranteed deletion of negative or fake reviews.
- The restaurant demands uncontrolled automatic posting.
- An active legal, food-safety, or public-relations crisis would dominate the experiment.
- Review volume is too low to test recurrence.
- The operator wants fabricated positive reviews or manipulation.

Record exclusions and reasons; they are market evidence.

## Cohort plan

| Segment | Target | Learning objective |
|---|---:|---|
| Independent dine-in | 4 | Core buyer and workflow |
| Delivery-heavy/cloud kitchen | 2 | Delivery complaints and platform fragmentation |
| Café/bakery | 2 | Higher-frequency, simpler-tone reviews |
| Premium/family restaurant | 1 | Brand sensitivity and approval caution |
| Agency-managed restaurant | 1 | Buyer/user separation and agency potential |

## Qualification score

Score each item 0, 1, or 2.

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Review frequency | <2/month | 2–4/month | >4/month |
| Unanswered-review pain | None | Occasional | Visible and recent |
| Decision-maker access | Unknown | Indirect | Direct |
| Listing complexity | One consistent source | Minor differences | Multiple likely inconsistencies |
| Existing workflow | Strong | Inconsistent | No owner |
| Willingness to pilot | Curiosity only | Meeting accepted | Real reviews + time committed |
| Payment signal | Refuses | Unclear | Deposit/pilot accepted |

**Priority rule:** pursue scores of 10 or more first. A low-scoring famous restaurant is less useful than a high-scoring independent restaurant.

## Required role map

Do not complete onboarding until these are named or explicitly marked unknown:

- Economic buyer
- Daily review operator
- Final reply approver
- Google/Zomato listing account holder
- Person responsible for operational fixes
- Backup contact

## Prospect evidence capture

For every prospect record:

- Public listing URLs
- Total review count
- Recent review frequency
- Count of recent unanswered reviews
- Existing reply style
- One concrete visible issue or opportunity
- Outreach angle used
- Date contacted
- Response and next step
- Qualification score

## Sample-size discipline

The goal is not a representative market survey. It is a learning-rich cohort capable of exposing workflow, trust, pricing, and operational friction. Do not add unqualified free users to make conversion numbers look better.
