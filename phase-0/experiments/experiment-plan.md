# Phase 0 Experiment Plan

## Experiment discipline

- Change one primary variable at a time where practical.
- Pre-register the expected outcome before contacting the cohort.
- Record every qualified prospect, including non-responses and refusals.
- Do not remove poor outcomes from denominators.
- Separate segment differences from message differences.

## E1 — Outreach angle

### Variants

A. Unanswered reviews
B. Listing inconsistency
C. Recurring complaint theme
D. Owner time saved

### Primary metric

Qualified prospect → snapshot accepted

### Secondary metrics

- Response rate
- Discovery call accepted
- Pilot accepted
- Paid commitment

### Decision

Select the angle with the strongest pilot/payment conversion, not merely the highest reply rate.

## E2 — Snapshot format

### Variants

A. One-page PDF or document
B. WhatsApp summary with evidence screenshots
C. Five-minute call walkthrough

### Metrics

- Time to consume
- Owner confirmation rate
- Concrete action rate
- Pilot conversion

## E3 — Delivery interface

### Variants

A. WhatsApp action card
B. Email action card
C. Lightweight web/shared form

### Metrics

- Median time to first action
- Approval completion rate
- Missed or forgotten actions
- Number of reminders required
- Stated preference after use

Observed completion outranks stated preference.

## E4 — Offer and price

Do not randomly vary price without recording qualification and segment.

### Offer A — Reply copilot

- Classification
- Draft replies
- Approval workflow
- Weekly summary
- Suggested range: ₹999–₹1,999/month/location

### Offer B — Reputation operations

- Offer A
- Listing snapshot/checks
- Recurring complaint detection
- Internal action recommendations
- Suggested range: ₹2,499–₹4,999/month/location

### Offer C — Managed service

- Daily monitoring
- Operator-led handling
- Owner approves sensitive or selected cases
- Monthly review
- Suggested range: ₹5,000+/month/location

### Commitment evidence

Record:

- Price shown
- Offer shown
- Buyer reaction
- Negotiated change
- Deposit/payment amount
- Invoice status
- Continuation date

No payment means no payment validation, even when the customer says the price is reasonable.

## E5 — Audit versus reply wedge

Randomly lead suitable prospects with either:

- Listing/reputation snapshot, or
- Three ready-to-use review drafts

Measure which starts a pilot and which predicts recurring usage.

## E6 — Internal action value

For alternating eligible complaints, compare:

- Reply draft only
- Reply draft plus specific internal action

Measure acknowledgement, assignment, completion, and continuation interview mentions. Never omit a safety-critical internal action for experimental purity.

## E7 — Reminder policy

Test:

- One same-day reminder
- One next-business-day reminder

Measure completion and annoyance. Do not use repeated chasing to manufacture activation.

## Operator economics

Record minutes for:

- Prospect research
- Snapshot preparation
- Onboarding
- Review normalisation
- Classification/drafting
- QA
- Customer follow-up
- Weekly report
- Sensitive escalation

Calculate per restaurant:

```text
monthly contribution estimate
= accepted monthly price
- projected model/tool cost
- projected operator cost
- support allowance
```

The objective is not a perfect forecast. It is to identify which manual step threatens the business.

## Experiment stop conditions

Pause an experiment when:

- safety or privacy is compromised;
- a customer misunderstands the service as automatic publishing;
- the test requires misleading outreach;
- sample assignment would withhold necessary handling;
- operational capacity prevents consistent delivery.

Document the reason and corrective action.
