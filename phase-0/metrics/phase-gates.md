# Phase 0 Gates

## Gate philosophy

Phase 0 passes only on observed customer behaviour. Documentation completeness, internal enthusiasm, synthetic test data, or technical feasibility cannot substitute for field evidence.

## Minimum evidence base

Before a final decision:

- 30 qualified prospects recorded
- 15 evidence-led outreach attempts
- 8 discovery conversations or documented refusals/non-response patterns sufficient to explain the gap
- 5 snapshots delivered
- 5 restaurants onboarded where possible
- 2 weeks of repeated workflow observation for active restaurants
- At least one concrete paid offer presented to every activated pilot

Missing a target does not automatically mean failure, but the decision record must explain why the evidence is still sufficient.

## Strong proceed gate

Proceed to Phase 1 when all safety requirements and most commercial/product requirements pass.

### Product behaviour

- At least 7 restaurants complete onboarding, or a smaller cohort supplies equivalent repeated evidence.
- At least 6 perform a real review/listing/internal action.
- At least 5 remain active in two separate weeks.
- At least 60% of sent drafts are published unchanged or with minor edits.
- Fewer than 15% are rejected as unusable.
- At least 70% of approved drafts are actually published, unless deliberate no-response decisions explain the gap.

### Action value

- At least 4 restaurants confirm or correct a material snapshot finding.
- At least 3 complete a listing or internal operational action.
- At least 2 identify recurring monitoring or issue intelligence as continuation value.

### Commercial

- At least 3 restaurants make a real financial commitment: payment, non-refundable deposit, or signed invoice with defined date.
- At least 2 request continuation without operator pressure.
- A plausible offer has positive projected contribution after model/tool and operator costs.

### Safety and trust

- Zero unresolved critical incidents.
- Every red case follows escalation policy.
- No public reply is posted automatically.
- Consent and deletion requests are handled and recorded.

## Conditional proceed / reposition

Proceed only with a changed thesis when evidence clusters around one of these outcomes.

### WhatsApp-first

- Customers repeatedly complete WhatsApp action cards.
- Dashboard/web preference is weak or contradicted by behaviour.
- Message workflow remains manageable and secure.

### Agency-first

- Agency-managed prospects convert materially better.
- One operator needs multiple restaurant workspaces.
- Restaurants defer buying and workflow to agencies.

### Audit-first service

- Snapshots generate payment and action.
- Recurring review workflow remains weak.
- Customers request periodic checks rather than daily software.

### Managed-service first

- Customers pay for outcomes but do not want to operate software.
- Human handling remains valuable and can support the price.

The final decision must name the repositioned buyer, workflow, offer, and next-phase acceptance gate.

## Stop or redesign gate

Do not proceed with the current thesis when several are true:

- Fewer than 3 restaurants repeatedly use any workflow.
- No real payment or deposit is secured.
- Most drafts require major rewrites.
- Approved drafts are seldom published.
- Snapshot findings are frequently trivial or wrong.
- Review volume cannot support recurring value.
- Human effort remains incompatible with accepted pricing.
- Sensitive-case risk exceeds the team’s operating capability.
- Customers value a problem that Tablevoice is not designed to solve.

## Safety veto

Commercial success cannot override:

- repeated privacy failures;
- inability to identify or contain sensitive cases;
- customer pressure for fabricated reviews or deceptive manipulation;
- workflow that publishes without clear authority;
- inability to separate customer records correctly.

## Phase 1 scope unlocked by a pass

A successful Phase 0 may unlock only the proven workflow:

- Manual/imported review inbox
- Structured restaurant and voice profiles
- Classification and risk policy
- Draft/approval/edit capture
- Copy-to-publish or deep-link workflow
- Basic listing-finding tracker
- Weekly action report
- Internal operator console

Google OAuth, automatic publishing, Zomato automation, pgvector, mobile apps, and multi-location agency architecture remain separate gates.
