# Draft Quality Checklist

Apply before every draft is sent to a customer.

## Source integrity

- [ ] Correct restaurant and location
- [ ] Exact review preserved
- [ ] Rating, date, platform, and language match the source
- [ ] Translation is marked as translation
- [ ] Cropped or incomplete evidence is identified

## Classification

- [ ] Primary category is supported by the text
- [ ] Risk is assigned independently from star rating
- [ ] Red triggers were checked explicitly
- [ ] Confidence reflects ambiguity
- [ ] Recommended disposition is appropriate

## Factuality

- [ ] No invented visit, order, staff, refund, investigation, or contact
- [ ] No assumption is presented as fact
- [ ] Unverified details are excluded or labelled
- [ ] Restaurant contact details are approved and current
- [ ] The reply does not claim “this never happens” or another unverifiable absolute

## Safety and authority

- [ ] No unauthorised refund, discount, replacement, or free-item promise
- [ ] No admission of legal liability
- [ ] No exposure of private customer or employee information
- [ ] No threat, retaliation, humiliation, or pressure to remove a review
- [ ] Sensitive cases use the escalation workflow

## Voice

- [ ] Matches the current voice constitution
- [ ] Uses the correct language and level of formality
- [ ] Avoids prohibited words and generic filler
- [ ] Length is appropriate to the review
- [ ] Does not repeat recent replies mechanically
- [ ] Refers to a real detail when doing so is safe

## Operational usefulness

- [ ] Public response strategy is explicit
- [ ] Internal action is specific and assignable
- [ ] Reply and internal action are not confused
- [ ] Missing context questions are necessary
- [ ] Recurrence is not claimed without enough observations

## Customer action card

- [ ] Main issue summary is accurate
- [ ] Risk and uncertainty are visible
- [ ] Draft is clearly separated from operator notes
- [ ] Approval choices are present
- [ ] No internal sensitive information appears in the public draft

## Final check

Read the draft from three positions:

1. **Customer:** Does this feel heard rather than managed?
2. **Restaurant owner:** Could this commit me to something I did not approve?
3. **Public observer:** Could this look defensive, careless, generic, or unsafe?

## Severity of defects

- **Critical:** privacy exposure, unsafe red-case handling, wrong restaurant, fabricated action, unauthorised compensation, legal-risk statement
- **Major:** wrong category, material factual assumption, damaging tone, unusable language, missing escalation
- **Minor:** awkward wording, unnecessary length, weak personalisation, formatting issue

Critical defects must be logged as incidents. Major defects count as rejected drafts even when corrected before publication, because they expose workflow risk.
