# Phase 0 Hypotheses

Every hypothesis must be falsifiable. Record evidence in `findings/experiment-log.md`; do not edit the hypothesis after seeing results without documenting the change.

## H1 — Review response is a recurring pain

**Claim:** Qualified restaurants receive enough reviews and experience enough uncertainty or delay that assisted review handling matters every week.

**Supporting evidence:**

- Owner identifies a current manual workflow and its cost.
- At least four qualified restaurants complete review actions in two separate weeks.
- Customers submit additional reviews without repeated operator chasing.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Review volume is too low for a recurring workflow.
- Owners consistently ignore ready-to-use drafts.
- The task is already handled adequately by staff or an agency.

## H2 — Draft-first earns sufficient trust

**Claim:** Owners will use AI-assisted drafts when assumptions, risk, internal action, and approval control are visible.

**Supporting evidence:**

- At least 60% of drafts are published unchanged or with minor edits.
- Fewer than 15% are rejected as unusable.
- Owners allow the workflow to handle both positive and negative reviews.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Major rewrites are common.
- Owners refuse public use even after onboarding.
- Replies are treated as generic content rather than trusted recommendations.

## H3 — Listing/reputation snapshot acquires customers

**Claim:** An evidence-backed snapshot opens conversations better than a generic AI pitch.

**Supporting evidence:**

- At least 25% of qualified snapshot recipients enter a pilot conversation.
- At least four pilot restaurants confirm or correct a material finding.
- At least three take a concrete listing or operational action.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Findings are trivial, obvious, or frequently wrong.
- Owners view the report but do not discuss or act on it.
- Generic time-saving outreach performs equally or better.

## H4 — There is subscription-shaped retention

**Claim:** Review handling, listing checks, or issue intelligence produces recurring rather than one-time value.

**Supporting evidence:**

- At least five restaurants remain active for two weeks.
- At least three request or accept paid continuation.
- Customers cite ongoing monitoring, not only initial cleanup, as the reason.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Value ends after the first batch of replies or audit.
- Customers request only occasional one-off help.
- Continued use requires constant manual prompting.

## H5 — The delivery interface can be identified

**Claim:** One primary workflow surface will emerge from observed completion behaviour.

Test:

- WhatsApp action card
- Email action card
- Lightweight web form or shared view

Record response latency, completion rate, missed actions, and owner preference. Do not interpret stated preference without completion behaviour.

## H6 — Unit economics can improve toward software margins

**Claim:** Concierge delivery exposes repeatable steps that can later be automated without removing necessary human judgement.

**Supporting evidence:**

- Median operator minutes per ordinary review falls across the pilot.
- Red cases remain rare enough for specialist handling.
- A plausible subscription price exceeds projected model, support, and review-handling cost.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Each restaurant requires unique bespoke service.
- Owner follow-up dominates delivery cost.
- Sensitive cases are frequent enough to require continuous expert intervention.

## H7 — A buyer will pay

**Claim:** At least one segment will make a real financial commitment.

Strong evidence, in descending order:

1. Paid monthly continuation
2. Paid pilot
3. Non-refundable deposit
4. Signed invoice with a defined payment date
5. Referral to another owner after using the service

Statements of willingness without commitment are interview data, not payment validation.

## H8 — The correct initial buyer is discoverable

Track these roles separately:

- Economic buyer
- Daily operator
- Public-reply approver
- Listing account holder
- Operational issue owner

**Possible outcomes:** restaurant-owner first, manager first, or agency first. Do not collapse these roles into “the owner.”

## H9 — Operational intelligence creates greater retention than writing alone

**Claim:** Recurring complaint detection and recommended internal actions are used, not merely admired.

**Supporting evidence:**

- At least four restaurants acknowledge a recurring theme.
- At least three assign or complete an internal action.
- Customers mention issue prevention in continuation interviews.

**Disconfirming evidence:**

- Customers only copy public replies.
- Internal actions are ignored.
- Recommendations lack enough context to be actionable.

## H10 — Safety controls are practical

**Claim:** The green/amber/red policy catches sensitive reviews without overwhelming ordinary work.

Measure:

- False-negative sensitive cases
- False-positive escalations
- Red-case rate
- Time to human review
- Owner satisfaction with escalation

A single serious unsafe draft is a critical incident and must be logged separately.
