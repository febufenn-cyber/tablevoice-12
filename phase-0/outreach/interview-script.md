# Interview Script

Use behaviour-first questions. Capture exact examples and dates where possible. Do not sell while the participant is describing the current workflow.

## Discovery interview — 20 minutes

### Current behaviour

1. Who checked your Google and Zomato reviews most recently?
2. Walk me through what happened from seeing the review to deciding whether to reply.
3. How many reviews did you answer last week or last month?
4. Show me a review that was difficult to answer. What made it difficult?
5. Which reviews do you intentionally ignore?
6. Has a review ever caused an internal staff conversation or operational change? What happened?
7. When public business information is wrong, how do you discover and correct it?
8. Who controls each listing account?
9. Do you currently pay an employee, freelancer, or agency for any of this work?
10. What is the consequence when no one handles it?

### Trust and safety

11. What would make you refuse to publish an AI-assisted reply?
12. Which topics must always reach you personally?
13. Can anyone promise a refund, replacement, discount, or free item publicly?
14. May employee names be mentioned?
15. What language and tone should a reply use?

### Workflow

16. Where would you act fastest: WhatsApp, email, or a web page?
17. Who gives final approval?
18. How quickly should positive, negative, and sensitive reviews be handled?
19. What information should accompany a draft before you trust it?
20. What would make a weekly report actionable rather than another message to ignore?

### Commitment

21. Will you provide three real reviews and 10–15 minutes of onboarding information?
22. Will you approve, edit, reject, or escalate each draft?
23. Will you manually publish approved replies and confirm publication?
24. Who else must approve a pilot?

## Mid-pilot interview

1. Which draft was most useful and why?
2. Which draft felt wrong or unsafe?
3. Show the most important edit you made. Why did you make it?
4. Did the risk label help or distract?
5. Did you use the internal action recommendation?
6. Was the chosen delivery channel convenient in practice?
7. Which reminder felt useful and which felt annoying?
8. What information did Tablevoice lack?
9. Which cases should never be automated?
10. Did you reply to more reviews than you normally would?

## Final interview

1. What would you miss if Tablevoice stopped tomorrow?
2. What would you do instead next month?
3. Which part deserves payment: replies, monitoring, listing checks, escalation, reports, or managed service?
4. Which part should be removed?
5. Who should be the daily user?
6. Would you connect the restaurant’s Google account after seeing the pilot? Why or why not?
7. What monthly amount feels easy to approve?
8. At what amount would another person need to approve the purchase?
9. Would you prefer software or a managed service?
10. Will you continue under the offered paid plan?
11. Can you introduce one relevant owner or agency operator?

## Interview evidence rules

- Separate direct quotes, observed behaviour, and interviewer inference.
- A hypothetical “yes” is not an activation or payment event.
- Record contradictions between what the participant says and does.
- Do not promise roadmap items during the interview.
- Obtain permission before recording or retaining personally identifying material.
